Digital Fabrication — Create | Save | Print | Assemble

Camp Instructor(s): Nicole Jacquard, SoA+D, IUB

Skill Level: Beginner - Advanced

Ratio Guarantee: 8 students per instructor, max 20 students per camp week.

Ages: 13 and up

Prerequisite: None

Software: Rhino

Location: Smith Research Center, Rm 120 see location.

Time: Monday - Friday, 9am - 4pm

Cost: $375

Course Description:

3D printing is breaking barriers between industry, institutions, designers and engineers. This next revolution in ‘making’ objects is only limited by one’s imagination. In this camp students will explore the software Rhinoceros (Rhino) in order to program objects that will be exported for 3D printing in a variety of materials. Participants will design and program several 3D objects, and as the workshop progresses, students will challenge themselves with more complex ideas.

What Students Take Home: Students take home several 3D printed objects including a printed replica of themselves.
